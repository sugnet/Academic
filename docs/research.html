My field of research is focused in general on multivariate data analysis, and more specifically biplots and graphical visualisation of multi-dimensional data. My research is both on theoretical developments of methods for visualisation of multi-dimensional data, R software developed to implement methods and applications leading to new extensions of current methodology.

After a 13-year stint in the corporate world, I moved to academia in July 2009 as associate professor at the University of Cape Town and in January 2017, I joined Stellenbosch University as professor in statistics. I am an elected member of the International Statistics Institute, served on the executive committees of the International Federation of Classification Societies and the International Association of Statistical Computing. I am also the chair of the Multivariate Data Analysis Special Interest Group in the South African Statistical Association.

In July 2011 we formalised our research group into the Centre for Multi-dimensional Data Visualisation (MuViSU) located in the Department of Statistics and Actuarial Science at Stellenbosch University. As director of MuViSU I am proud to say we have a network of national and international members as well as post graduate students working on a variety of collaborative research projects.
